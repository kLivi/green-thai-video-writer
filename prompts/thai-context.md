## Solar Installations

### Permits & Grid Connection
- PEA (Provincial Electricity Authority) handles rural areas; MEA (Metropolitan Electricity Authority) handles Bangkok, Nonthaburi, Samut Prakan
- Net metering: PEA/MEA buy-back rates vary — typically 2.20 THB/kWh for residential (check current rates)
- Rooftop solar <10 kWp: simplified permit process through PEA/MEA
- Larger systems: need ERC (Energy Regulatory Commission) license
- Building permit may be needed if structural modifications required

### Typical Costs (2025-2026 reference)
- On-grid solar rooftop: 30,000-45,000 THB per kWp installed (residential)
- Off-grid with battery: 50,000-80,000 THB per kWp
- Labor rates: 15-25% of total system cost
- Inverter replacement: plan for replacement at year 10-12

### Government Incentives
- BOI (Board of Investment) tax incentives for commercial solar
- DEDE (Department of Alternative Energy) programs
- Note: Always verify if incentives are still active — Thai energy policy changes frequently
- Cabinet approvals vs Royal Gazette publication distinction

## EV Charging
- PEA VOLTA and MEA charging networks
- EA Anywhere (Energy Absolute) network — largest private network
- PTT EV Station — expanding at gas stations
- Home charging: 220V single-phase standard in Thailand
- 3-phase power available but requires PEA/MEA application

## Wind Power
- Best wind resources: Nakhon Ratchasima, Chaiyaphum, Nakhon Si Thammarat, Songkhla
- Average wind speeds in Thailand are modest (4-6 m/s at hub height in best areas)
- Most projects in the 10-90 MW range

## Floating Solar
- EGAT's Sirindhorn Dam project (45 MW) — flagship reference
- Thailand's reservoir surface area creates significant floating solar potential
- Challenges: water level fluctuation, anchoring, maintenance access

## General Context
- Thailand electricity tariff: progressive rate structure, ~4-5 THB/kWh for residential (varies by usage tier)
- Peak demand surcharges apply
- Thai climate: high solar irradiance (~1,400-1,600 kWh/m²/year), monsoon season affects output
- Rainy season (May-October) reduces solar output 20-30%
